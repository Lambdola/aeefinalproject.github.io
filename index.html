    A closer look into the department.

    VISION

    To achieve and sustain self-sufficiency in food and fibre production theough Engineering

    MISSION

    The mission of the Department is to provide educaton in the application of basic engineering principles for the production, design and utilization of agricultural and natural resources for self-sufficiency.

    BRIEF HISTORY

    The Department of Agricultural and Environmental Engmeering (previously known as Agricultural Engineering) is one of the four foundational departments established when the former Institute of Applied Science and Technology acquired Faculty Status in 1975.

    Prior to this, elements which now form the Department were operating within the Institute. The degree of B.Sc. Agricultural Engineering was among the approved degree programmes for the Institute at its commencement in 1972. The first set of graduands passed out in June 1976.

    The Department now offers B.Sc. degree in Agricultural and Environmental Engineering at the undergraduate level and also runs approved Postgraduate programmes up to Ph.D. level. This programme helps prepare students for careers involving the application of engineering principles to agricultural production, food processing and storage, agricultural structures, conservation of land and water resources, and the environment.

    PHILOSOPHY

    For quick agricultural development and prosperity of a nation, skilled engineering manpower is unavoidable.

    The broad philosophy of the Department of Agricultural and Environmental Engineering is to produce high graduates with high academic standards and sound practical exposure to meet the continuous requirements of rapid agricultural development and alleviation of environmental degradation brought about by industrial development, widespread urbanization, impact of human activities and climate change.

    In pursuance of the aforementioned, the B.Sc. Agricultural and Environmental Engineering Programme has been designed to rflect teaching and research focus in the following areas:

    • ⋇Farm Power, Machinery and Mechanization of Agricultural Operation as well as precision Agriculture
    • ⋇Soil and Water Conservation, Irrigation, Drainage and Erosion Control in Agricultural Lands.
    • ⋇Agricultural Crop Processing, Storage, and Food Process Engineering
    • ⋇Design, Control and Modelling of Natural and Agricultural Processes through the application of Electronics, Instrumentation, Information Technology and Computational Methods.
    • ⋇Environmental Engineering with aspects of Pollution Monitoring, Analysis Design of waste handling and disposal systems and Environmental Impact Assessment.
    • ⋇Farm Structures and Environment, Controlled Environment Agriculture, Structural Composite Development
    • ⋇Rural and Farm Electrification

    The programme accommodates common foundational engineering and mathematical courses at the initial stage to provide broad-based engineering training followed by special skills and in-depth study in areas mentioned above. The Department actively encourages inter-disciplinary and collaborative research and training with other Departments within and outside the Faculty, as well as in Nigeria and overseas.

    OBJECTIVES

    B.Sc. AGRICULTURAL AND ENVIRONMENTAL ENGINEERING PROGRAMME

    The objectives of the programme in Agricultural and Environmental Engineering are:

    (a) To produce graduates with sufficient knowledge to apply engineering principles to the practice of agriculture including mechanization, crop preservation and storage, irrigation and drainage, farm structures, controlled environment agriculture and rural electrification.

    (b) To train engineers with sufficiently broad-based knowledge to tackle day-to-day agricultural engineering problems and continuously relate their acquired skills to the needs of the society.

    (c) To produce graduates through a well-established training facility and curriculum which reflect thei professionalism in Agricultural Engineering Practivce.

    (d) To produce graduates that are versatile in the operations, maintenance and repair of labour-saving equipment that are used in agriculture and sysytems/processes that minimize environmental degradation.

    (e) To prepare students for careers involving the application of engineering principeles to agricultural production, provision of agricultural structures, foof processing and conservation of land and water resources.
